06. Self Intro

First-Principles AI Research & Engineering

I am a Master of Computer Science student at Rice University, driven by a commitment to understand machine learning from first principles. Rather than treating Large Language Models as black boxes, my focus lies in their interpretability, weight evolution, and the mathematical essence of feature representations. This foundational perspective allows me to bridge the gap between academic frontiers and production-scale architectures.

I advocate for the deep fusion of multimodal capabilities—seeking to build intelligent systems that are intuitive, robust, and transparent, rather than merely chasing baseline metrics.

  • Large Language Models: Expertise in model fine-tuning (qLoRA, LUPI), test-time reasoning (TTFT), and efficient inference deployment (vLLM).
  • RAG Search & Retrieval: Architecting high-throughput semantic search systems using FAISS, BM25, and Knowledge Graphs.
  • AI Agentic Systems: Designing multi-agent, stateful workflows via LangChain, ReAct, and LangGraph to perform complex autonomous tasks.
